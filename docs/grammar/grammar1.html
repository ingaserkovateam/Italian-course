⚖️ Gender of Nouns

In Italian, all nouns have a grammatical gender: masculine or feminine. This affects the articles and adjectives used with them.

🟢 Common Endings

  • -o → usually masculine (e.g. il libro (the book) – the book)
  • -a → usually feminine (e.g. la casa (the house) – the house)
  • -e → can be masculine or feminine

🔍 What about -e?

Words ending in -e can be either masculine or feminine, and their gender must be learned together with the article. Here are some examples:

  • Masculine: il ristorante (restaurant), il dente (tooth), il giornale (newspaper)
  • Feminine: la chiave (key), la notte (night), la classe (class)

📝 Tip: Always learn new nouns with their article (e.g. la mano, not just mano).

⚠️ Common Exceptions

  • Masculine: il problema (problem), il sistema (system), il tema (essay)
  • Feminine: la mano (hand), la foto (photo, from fotografia)

📰 Articles

Italian articles agree with the gender and number of the noun. Here's a simplified guide to the most common definite articles:

Before consonant Before s+consonant / z / ps / gn / x / y Before vowel
Masculine Singular il libro (the book) lo studente (the student) (the student) l’amico (the friend) (the friend)
Masculine Plural i libri (the books) gli studenti (the students) (the students) gli amici (the friends) (the friends)
Feminine Singular la casa (the house) — l’amica (the (female) friend)
Feminine Plural le case (the houses) — le amiche (the (female) friends)

📌 Notes

lo is used with masculine words that start with:

  • s + consonant → lo studente (the student) (the student)
  • z → lo zaino (the backpack)
  • gn → lo gnomo (the gnome)
  • ps, pn, x, y → lo psicologo (the psychologist), lo xilofono (the xylophone)

l’ is used before vowels: l’amico (the friend) (the friend), l’isola (the island)

gli is the plural of both lo and l’ (masculine).

📝 Tip: Learn each noun with its article to build good habits early.

🔍 Indefinite Articles (a/an)

In Italian, indefinite articles also agree with gender and follow certain pronunciation rules:

  • un → masculine, before most consonants and vowels
    🧾 un libro (a book), un amico (a (male) friend)
  • uno → masculine, before s+consonant, z, gn, ps, x, y
    🧾 uno studente (a student), uno psicologo (a psychologist)
  • una → feminine, before consonants
    🧾 una casa (a house)
  • un’ → feminine, before vowels
    🧾 un’amica (a (female) friend)
