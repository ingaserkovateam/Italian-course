✍️ Essere – To Be

📌 Essere is one of the most essential verbs in Italian. It means “to be” and is used constantly in everyday conversations.

You use essere to:

  • Introduce yourself: Io sono Elisa. – I am Elisa.
  • Talk about where you are from: Tu sei di Milano? – Are you from Milan?
  • Describe your job: Lei è insegnante. – She is a teacher.
  • Express emotions or conditions: Noi siamo stanchi. – We are tired.
  • Describe location (with a complement): La casa è a Firenze.

📌 Essere changes form depending on who is speaking. It is important to recognize the endings, because the subject is often not said — it's "understood" from the verb.

⚠️ Be careful with è (he/she/it is) — it has an accent! Don't confuse it with e (which means “and”).
