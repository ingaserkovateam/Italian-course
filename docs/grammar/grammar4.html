✍️ Avere – To Have

📌 Avere means "to have" in Italian and is another core verb you'll use very often.

We use avere to talk about:

  • Possession: Ho un gatto. – I have a cat.
  • Age: Ho 30 anni. – I’m 30 years old.
  • Physical states: Hai fame? – Are you hungry?
  • Emotions: Lui ha paura. – He is afraid.

🤔 What's the difference between *essere* and *avere* with emotions?

Both verbs can describe emotions or feelings, but they are used in different ways:

  • Essere + adjective → describes a mood or emotional state
    • Sono felice. – I’m happy.
    • Lui è stanco. – He is tired.
    • Siamo tristi. – We are sad.
  • Avere + noun → expresses sensations or emotional needs
    • Ho fame. – I’m hungry.
    • Hai paura? – Are you afraid?
    • Lei ha freddo. – She feels cold.
📌 TIP: Use essere with adjectives (happy, tired). Use avere with “noun feelings” (hunger, fear, thirst).

🔁 In Italian, we use avere for some emotions and physical sensations, unlike English. These are fixed expressions that use “to have”.

  • Ho fame. – I’m hungry (lit. I have hunger)
  • Hai sete? – Are you thirsty?
  • Lei ha paura. – She is afraid
  • Abbiamo fretta. – We are in a hurry
📌 Don’t try to translate word by word. Just learn them as expressions!
⚠️ Just like *essere*, *avere* changes with the subject pronoun!
