To conjugate a verb in Italian means to change its ending depending on the subject. All Italian verbs in the infinitive end in -are, -ere, or -ire.

Stem = the part before the ending. Example:

  • parlare ‚Üí parl- + are
  • credere ‚Üí cred- + ere
